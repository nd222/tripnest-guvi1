DELHI

Delhi, the capital city of India, is a vibrant blend of history and modernity. As one of the oldest cities in the world, Delhi has seen the rise and fall of various empires, each leaving behind a rich cultural and architectural legacy. The city is divided into two parts: Old Delhi, with its narrow lanes and historical monuments like the Red Fort and Jama Masjid, and New Delhi, which is characterized by its wide avenues, colonial-era buildings, and government institutions. Delhi‚Äôs charm lies in its ability to seamlessly merge the old with the new, offering a unique experience to visitors.

The city is home to a plethora of museums that reflect its diverse cultural heritage. The National Museum, one of the largest in India, houses an extensive collection of artifacts ranging from the prehistoric era to modern times. The museum's exhibits include ancient manuscripts, sculptures, and decorative arts from various periods of Indian history. The Indian Museum of Natural History, another major attraction, offers insights into the natural world with its vast collection of flora and fauna, fossils, and geological samples.

In addition to these, Delhi also boasts several specialized museums that cater to different interests. The National Gallery of Modern Art showcases a vast collection of contemporary Indian art, while the Gandhi Smriti and Nehru Memorial Museum offer a glimpse into the lives of India‚Äôs iconic leaders. The Crafts Museum, with its traditional textiles and crafts from across India, provides a platform for artisans to showcase their skills.

National Museum: New Delhi

National Museum is considered to be one of the largest and most comprehensive museums in India, the National Museum in Delhi offers an extensive collection that covers 5,000 years of Indian history. Its exhibits range from prehistoric artifacts to contemporary art, showcasing sculptures, manuscripts, decorative arts, arms and armor, and more. Gandhi Smriti Musuem

Gandhi Smriti Museum is also known as Birla House, Gandhi Smriti is the site where Mahatma Gandhi spent the last 144 days of his life and was assassinated in 1948.
